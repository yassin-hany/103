Smart Home Hub Troubleshooting

1.1 Hub does not power on
    - Verify the power adapter is firmly connected to both the hub and the
      wall outlet.
    - Try a different wall outlet to rule out a dead circuit.
    - If the LED indicator does not light up at all, the power adapter may
      be defective. Contact support for a replacement adapter.

1.2 Hub loses Wi-Fi connection frequently
    - Ensure the hub is within 10 meters of your wireless router.
    - Check that your router firmware is up to date.
    - Avoid placing the hub near microwave ovens, cordless phones, or other
      devices that operate on the 2.4 GHz band.
    - If the issue persists, perform a factory reset by holding the reset
      button for 10 seconds, then reconnect to your network.

1.3 Hub does not discover connected devices
    - Confirm that all devices are on the same local network as the hub.
    - Restart the hub by unplugging it for 30 seconds, then plugging it
      back in.
    - Check that device firmware is current using the Northwind companion
      app under Settings > Device Updates.

Wireless Speaker Troubleshooting

2.1 No audio output
    - Verify the speaker is charged (LED should show solid green).
    - Confirm Bluetooth pairing: open your device's Bluetooth settings and
      check that "NW-Speaker" appears as connected.
    - If paired but silent, check the volume level on both the speaker and
      the source device.

2.2 Audio distortion or crackling
    - Move the speaker closer to the paired device to improve the Bluetooth
      signal.
    - Reduce the source volume to 80% or lower — distortion at maximum
      volume indicates the source signal is clipping.
    - If distortion occurs at all volume levels, perform a firmware update
      through the Northwind companion app.

Portable Power Station Troubleshooting

3.1 Power station does not charge
    - Use only the included 65W USB-C charger. Third-party chargers below
      45W will not charge the unit.
    - Check the charging port for debris and clean with compressed air if
      needed.
    - If the battery indicator flashes red, the internal temperature may be
      too high. Allow the unit to cool for 30 minutes before retrying.

3.2 Connected devices do not receive power
    - Verify the output port is enabled: press the power button once to
      cycle through DC, USB-A, and USB-C outputs.
    - Check that the connected device does not exceed the rated output
      (500W continuous, 1000W peak).
    - If the power station shuts down under load, the overcurrent protection
      has triggered. Disconnect the device and check its power draw.
